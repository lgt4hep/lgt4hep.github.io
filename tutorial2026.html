LGT4HEP: 2026 Tutorials


In Spring 2026, we host 3-days of in-person tutorials + USQCD All-Hands Meeting 2026 at Argonne National Laboratory, during April 15-17 2026; the lectures tutorials are listed as following:

Optimal hardware for QCD and hardware-aware high-performance software

Peter Boyle (he/him)

Senior Scientist
Physics Department
Brookhaven National Laborarory

Email: pboyle@bnl.gov

Lecture bio: I'm interested in Lattice gauge theory with a focus on high energy physics and weak matrix elements. Also interested in LQCD software, algorithms, machines. Author of the Grid library for LQCD.

Lecture breif description: Tutorial covers a wide range of algorithms and software approaches of LQCD, focussing on simple examples in the Grid library.

ML/AI for lattice QCD

Dan Hackett (he/him)

Research Associate
Theory Division
Fermilab

Email: dhackett@fnal.gov

Lecture bio: I work on lattice QFT/QCD and AI/ML applications to theoretical physics, mainly applied to hadron and nuclear structure for DUNE and the EIC. I've also been thinking about NNQFTs and entanglement and have previously worked on lattice applications for BSM physics and of quantum computing. In my spare time, I read a lot of SF/F and I like to cook!

Lecture breif description: I will discuss AI/ML applications to lattice QCD. Main topics covered will include normalizing flows to accelerate lattice calculations, and how to use agentic AI in lattice QCD.
